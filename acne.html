What are the types of Acne?

1. Blackheads: Plugged follicles that reach the surface of the skin and open up. They look black because the air discolors the sebum not because they are dirty.
2. Whiteheads: Plugged hair follicles that stay beneath the skin and produce a white bump.
3. Papules: Inflamed lesions that usually appears as small, pink bump and can be tender to touch.
4. Pustules: Papules topped by white or yellow pus filled lesions that may be red at the base.
5. Nodules: Large, painful, solid lesions that are lodged deep within the skin.

What are the Causes of Acne?

1. Unbalanced hormones cause acne vulgaris, causing oil glands to produce too much sebum, leading to skin cell rupturing and pore obstruction by P. acnes, the acne-causing bacterium.
2. Stress exacerbates acne by enlarging capillaries, increasing blood flow, causing redness and irritation, raising testosterone levels in adrenal glands, and increasing sebaceous oil gland secretion.
3. Pollution in the environment causes skin pores to block, leading to acne, a skin condition characterized by inflammation of clogged pores caused by various chemical gases.
4. Overuse of cosmetics, such as foundation and concealer, can clog pores, worsen acne, and hinder skin breathing, leading to acne.
5. Acne can be exacerbated by certain medications, including steroids, iodide, bromide, anticonvulsant medications, oral contraceptives, and tablets for conditions like PCOS.
