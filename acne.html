What is Acne?

Acne is a pore-related disorder where skin pores quickly fill with dead cells, leading to clogged pores due to the body's inability to clean them, unlike healthy pores.

What are the Symptoms of Acne?

Acne symptoms include redness, sensitivity, and sensitivity, which can be identified by dermatologists who can then plan a suitable treatment plan.

1. Blackheads are clogged pores due to sebum and skin debris build-up, with the top remaining open and the rest blocked, giving the pore a black appearance.

2. Whiteheads, caused by sebum and dead skin cells clogging pores, resemble small lumps sticking out of the skin and are more difficult to treat than blackheads.

3. Papules, pink, delicate pore clogs, are formed when skin pores collapse due to acute inflammation, causing skin damage.

4. Pustules, filled with pus, are red with a yellow or white head on top, resembling a red bump emerging from the skin.

5. Nodules, caused by a clogged, enlarged pore, are harder to cure than pustules and papules due to their deeper location in the skin.

6. Cysts, painful bumps, are red, white, or both, caused by blocked pores from germs, sebum, and dead skin cells, and are the most severe form of acne.

What are the Causes of Acne?

Acne, a condition causing breakouts, can occur on various parts of the skin, including the face, back, neck, chest, buttocks, and arms.

1. Unbalanced hormones cause acne vulgaris, causing oil glands to produce too much sebum, leading to skin cell rupturing and pore obstruction by P. acnes, the acne-causing bacterium.

2. Stress exacerbates acne by enlarging capillaries, increasing blood flow, causing redness and irritation, raising testosterone levels in adrenal glands, and increasing sebaceous oil gland secretion.

3. Pollution in the environment causes skin pores to block, leading to acne, a skin condition characterized by inflammation of clogged pores caused by various chemical gases.

4. Overuse of cosmetics, such as foundation and concealer, can clog pores, worsen acne, and hinder skin breathing, leading to acne.

5. Acne can be exacerbated by certain medications, including steroids, iodide, bromide, anticonvulsant medications, oral contraceptives, and tablets for conditions like PCOS.
