Chemical Peel Treatment at Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic

Everyone desires the flawless skin beyond the outermost layer of the skin.

The outer epidermal layer, composed of dead skin cells, protects the inner dermal layer from external factors, causing dull, hyper-pigmented skin and acne proneness.

Chemical Peels are a solution for removing wrinkles, hyper-pigmentation, and acne by exfoliating the dead skin layer.

What is Chemical Peeling?

Chemical peels, also known as derma peeling or chemo-exfoliation, are a non-invasive method to improve facial skin texture by removing the top layer, resulting in a younger, healthier appearance. They can be used on various body parts, including the face, for aging, sun exposure, cosmetic overuse, and acne.

How does it works?

Chemical peels involve exfoliation, skin flaw removal, and regeneration, destroying the outer layer of the skin through chemical reactions.

Who is an ideal candidate for Chemical Peel Treatment at Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic?

Ideal candidates for Chemical Peel treatment at Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic include individuals with light hair or fair complexion, those seeking to improve skin texture, sun-damaged skin, acne keratosis, or uneven pigmentation.

However, those nursing or pregnant, with skin conditions like psoriasis, rosacea, dermatitis, or those with skin infections, ailments, cuts, or burns should avoid chemical peels.

What happens during a Chemical Peel treatment?

Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic offers chemical peel treatments, which involve thorough cleaning, anesthesia, and application of a pre-made chemical solution. The treatment may cause discomfort, tingling, burning, or itching. Doctors may use a saline compress, ointment, or wash off the peel, depending on the type. The skin may be covered and left alone for a day. Chemical peel procedures can last from 30 minutes to 90 minutes.

What is the recovery period after Chemical Peeling like?

The recovery period for chemical peel treatment involves itching, discomfort, and redness, which typically subside within a few days. Medication is administered to alleviate pain and irritation. It's advised to avoid cosmetics for a few days and use a light moisturizer alongside sunscreen.

What are the benefits of Chemical Peel treatment at Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic?

Chemical Peel treatment at Lotus Healthcare and Aesthetic Clinic offers numerous benefits, including reducing pigmentation, acne, removing imperfections, reducing sun damage, reducing light blotchiness, promoting a youthful appearance, removing fine wrinkles, preventing dry skin and acne scars, and reducing deep wrinkles.
